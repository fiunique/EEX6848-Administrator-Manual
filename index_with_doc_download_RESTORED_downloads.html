I Vision and Moral Purpose

The evaluation of students suspected of having disabilities is not a compliance exercise; it is a moral commitment to equitable access to education. The Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA) guarantees a Free Appropriate Public Education (FAPE). Administrators are responsible for ensuring that evaluation systems are timely, comprehensive, culturally responsive, and instructionally meaningful.

"Fullan's knowledge-building framework positions leadership as the creation of systems where information becomes shared professional knowledge."

Evaluation reports must not function as static compliance artifacts. They must serve as organizational learning tools that:

  • Clarify instructional needs
  • Improve intervention fidelity
  • Guide resource allocation
  • Strengthen tiered systems of support

Core Principle: The administrator's moral purpose is to ensure that evaluation practices improve student outcomes while protecting procedural integrity.

C. Timelines and Consent

Florida Rule 6A-6.0331

The district must request consent within 30 calendar days of:

  • ‚Ä¢ Parent request
  • ‚Ä¢ Inadequate response to intensive intervention
  • Evaluations must be sufficiently comprehensive
  • Reevaluations occur every three years unless waived

Administrators must monitor timeline compliance through SIS and EdPlan documentation systems.

D. Comprehensive Evaluation Standards

A comprehensive evaluation must:

  • Review attendance and intervention history
  • Include multiple assessment tools
  • Avoid reliance on a single measure
  • Address all suspected areas of disability

Important: SLD, LI, and E/BD require documented PS/RtI intervention data prior to or concurrent with referral.

E. Parent Participation and Procedural Safeguards

Administrators must ensure:

  • 10-day written notice of IEP meetings
  • Documentation of contact attempts
  • Provision of Procedural Safeguards annually and at required trigger points

Meaningful parent participation must be documented in conference notes.

III Evidence-Based Evaluation Practices

"The Council for Exceptional Children defines evidence-based practices (EBPs) as practices supported by high-quality experimental research, replication, and representative participant samples."

Administrators must ensure:

  • Assessment tools are psychometrically valid
  • Interventions are implemented with fidelity
  • Eligibility decisions are grounded in high-quality data

Decision-Making Protocol

Phase Action Evidence Required
1. Screening Universal screening data review Benchmark assessments, teacher observations
2. Intervention Tiered intervention implementation Progress monitoring data, fidelity checks
3. Referral Formal evaluation referral PS/RtI documentation, parent consent
4. Evaluation Comprehensive assessment Multiple measures across domains
5. Eligibility Team determination Evaluation report, eligibility criteria
